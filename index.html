About

I’m a computational scientist specializing in computational physics and chemistry, accelerated AI/machine learning methods for science, and HPC-optimized algorithms. My work spans molecular dynamics (MD), density functional theory (DFT), real-time density matrix dynamics, and modern deep learning (transformers, geometric deep learning, diffusion/flow models).

At Los Alamos National Laboratory, I develop ML-enabled workflows and models that shorten the loop from hypothesis to simulation to validation—especially for materials design and biomolecular interaction modeling.

Now

  • Materials discovery agentic workflow: literature → simulation (MD/DFT) → review/correction.
  • Protein interaction modeling: geometric transformers + protein language models.
  • Physics-informed ML for electron and charge dynamics in nanomaterials.

Protein Binding Site Prediction (Surface Graphs + Transformers + Protein LLMs)

Developed a fast binding-site prediction method on protein pair surfaces using coarse surface geometry graphs, geometric transformers, and protein language model embeddings; achieved ~6× speedup and ~8% accuracy improvement vs prior surface-based approaches.

PyTorchPyTorch GeometricGeometric TransformersProtein LLMs

Code: LANL GitLab (private) · Manuscript in prep

Physics-Informed ML for Plasmon / Charge Dynamics in Metal Nanoparticles

Built physics-informed time-series ML models (GNN-based) to predict electron trajectories in nanoparticles, delivering ~10,000× speedup compared to real-time TDDFT while preserving key dynamical features.

PyTorchGNNsTime-seriesNanoplasmonics

ML Force Field for Ionic Liquids (Active Learning + Uncertainty)

Developed a graph neural network force field using active learning and ensemble uncertainty. Enabled large-scale MD of complex ionic liquids, including million-atom scale simulations.

GPU Mixed-Precision Inverse Factorization Solver (Tensor Cores)

Implemented a GPU-optimized iterative matrix inverse solver leveraging mixed precision on NVIDIA tensor cores; achieved ~3× speedup over conventional solvers for scalable quantum molecular dynamics.
