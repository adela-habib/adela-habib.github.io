About

Overview of my research focus and what I enjoy working on: combining first-principles simulations, machine learning, and scalable computing to accelerate scientific discovery.

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Los Alamos National Laboratory working across the Bioscience and Theoretical divisions. My interests center on building accelerated AI/ML methods and high-performance simulation workflows for materials discovery and biomolecular applications.

I particularly enjoy projects that connect rigorous physics/chemistry with practical software: from GPU-optimized numerical linear algebra to graph neural networks that learn force fields or time-dependent electron dynamics.

Research interests

  • AI agents for materials discovery (literature → simulation → validation loop)
  • Geometric deep learning & protein language models for protein interaction prediction
  • Machine-learned interatomic potentials / force fields
  • Real-time electronic/spin dynamics from first principles
  • Mixed-precision GPU algorithms for electronic structure calculations
