About

I’m a computational scientist focused on building reliable, fast scientific ML systems and physics-based simulation methods. My work spans quantum chemistry and electronic-structure theory, molecular dynamics, time-dependent charge and spin dynamics, and GPU-accelerated numerical linear algebra.

Core strengths

Scientific ML for atoms, materials, and proteins Active learning + uncertainty HPC kernels (CUDA, tensor cores) Agentic workflows for simulation + literature Real-time density matrix dynamics DFT / excited-state methods

Research

I develop accelerated approaches for scientific discovery across materials and biomolecular systems. Recent directions include agentic workflows that connect literature search to simulation and validation, geometric deep learning for protein interaction modeling, physics-informed ML for charge dynamics in nanoparticles, and GPU-optimized solvers for electronic-structure calculations.
